Quick note before we get into any of this, you won't see my actual name attached to the org and I wanted to flag that upfront so it doesn't read as weird or evasive when you notice it. My day job and my family are both pretty closely tied to my real name, and the moment you put a face on an org it changes how everyone reads everything, what it's about, who it represents, all of that, so I'm just keeping the personal side of it out of the picture and letting the org speak for itself. The "I" you'll see in this post and the ones going forward is still a real person writing it, it's just a real person writing as the org rather than as themselves, and I hope that lands as a reasonable trade for being able to talk a little more honestly about the rest of this.

My background is in electrical engineering, this whole thing is genuinely a passion project I'm running on the side of an actual career, and the honest long-term hope is to eventually build something that feels even a fraction as good as what NaVi was during their CS:GO era, the way they ran fans, players, content, the brand, all of it, even though I genuinely have no expectations about whether that takes a year or ten years or never quite gets all the way there.

I grew up playing Counter-Strike and Call of Duty, and most of what I learned about esports as a whole came from staying up at 3am during high school watching CS finals from whatever LAN was running that week, where somewhere along the way I noticed I was actually way more interested in the org side of things than I was in the idea of being on stage myself, and the part about building one of these things always pulled at me more than the part about playing in one ever did.

Overwatch I actually came to pretty embarrassingly late, I only downloaded it back in April of this year, mostly because a couple of friends in a totally separate Discord wouldn't shut up about it for weeks, and somewhere between starting out on DPS and grinding my way up to Diamond at a slightly suspicious pace, the back of my brain very quietly went *oh, this is the next thing*, and I started looking at the OWCS Pacific landscape more seriously than was probably healthy for somebody who'd been playing the game for a month.

The actual founding kind of started by accident, I'd been hanging out in the r/overwatch Discord server and ended up in a long back-and-forth conversation with [Kenobi](https://x.com/obiwank52854234), and at some point he mentioned, almost as an aside, that owning a team in OWCS Pacific wasn't anywhere near as out of reach as I'd been quietly assuming, which honestly cracked the whole thing open for me, because up until that conversation I'd been operating on the assumption that an org slot in any tier-one circuit was either inherited from somewhere or backed by a venture round somebody else had closed, and that's just not the case in Pacific the way it is in some of the other regions.

From there it turned into a few weeks of cold-messaging random players off the top 500 leaderboard and asking if their team actually had ownership behind it and whether anything was on the table, which mostly produced a long list of dead ends and a handful of polite "no thanks" replies, until eventually a representative from Rankers got back to me, we talked back and forth over a couple of weeks, and on May 5th I officially acquired the roster, which is how the same lineup that finished out Stage 1 ends up carrying forward into Stage 2 under a brand new org.

That's basically the whole story, there are no investors, no PR firm, no parent company sitting behind any of this, just one person who grew up watching CS finals at strange hours of the night, downloaded Overwatch about six weeks ago, and somehow ended up deciding that owning a team was a reasonable thing to take on this year.

If we end up somewhere in the ballpark of what NaVi looked like at their CS:GO peak, that would obviously be incredible and probably take a decade, and if we end up looking more like NaVi at the very beginning, scrappy and small and figuring it out as we go, that's honestly fine too, because everyone starts somewhere and I'd rather start small and earn the rest than skip any of the parts that actually matter.

GLHF.

The Najdorf Esports team
