I'm not going to put my name on this. My day job and my family are both tied to my real name, and the second you attach a person to an org you change the conversation. So you'll just get the org, and from here on the "I" in this post is a person you'll never see on a roster card.

My background is electrical engineering. This is a passion project. The honest goal is to be at the level NaVi was during their CS:GO era, the way they ran fans, players, content, all of it. I don't expect that to happen on any kind of schedule. It's the target.

I grew up playing Counter-Strike and Call of Duty. Most of what I learned about esports as a thing came from watching CS finals at 3am during high school. I never had the dream of playing in one of those. I had the dream of putting one on.

Overwatch I came to late. I downloaded it in April of this year, mostly because a couple of friends in another Discord wouldn't stop talking about it. I started on DPS, climbed to Diamond pretty fast, and somewhere in that climb the back of my brain went *oh, this is the next thing*.

The actual founding happened in the r/overwatch Discord server. I struck up a conversation with [Kenobi](https://x.com/obiwank52854234), and at one point he mentioned that owning a team in OWCS Pacific isn't as out of reach as it sounds. That was the unlock. I'd assumed an org slot in any tier-one circuit was either inherited or backed by a venture round. It isn't, not in Pacific.

From there it was a few weeks of cold-messaging people off the top 500 leaderboard and asking if their team had ownership. A lot of dead ends. Eventually a representative from Rankers answered, we talked back and forth for a couple of weeks, and on May 5th I officially acquired the roster. The same lineup that finished Stage 1 carries forward into Stage 2 under a new org.

That's the whole story. No investors, no PR firm, no parent company. One person who grew up watching CS finals, downloaded Overwatch six weeks ago, and decided that owning a team was a reasonable thing to do this year.

If we end up looking like NaVi at their CS:GO peak, great. If we look like NaVi at the start, that's fine too. Everyone starts somewhere.

GLHF.

The Najdorf Esports team
