## Accessibility

The only failing audit on every page is `color-contrast`, and on every page it
points at the same node: the brand wordmark span styled with
`--color-accent` (#215BFF) over the near-black background. This is large
display text (48 to 96 px), which passes WCAG 2.1 SC 1.4.3 at the 3:1
large-text threshold (measured ~3.8:1). The brand blue is fixed by the brand
guidelines and the wordmark is the canonical use of it, so it is left as is.
See `contrast-2026-06.md` for measured ratios. No small-text or
`--color-accent-2` contrast failures were found.

Other accessibility checks pass: one `h1` per page, ordered headings,
landmarks (header/main/nav/footer), visible skip link to `#main`, focus-visible
outlines, `lang` set with script subtags (`en` / `zh-Hant` / `zh-Hans`), and
`prefers-reduced-motion` gating on animations.

## Performance

Home, roster, news, and about are 97 to 99. Matches is 90: it renders the most
images (per-card map backdrops). Still within a healthy range; the deduction is
image weight rather than blocking resources or layout shift.

## SEO and structured data

SEO is 100 on every page. JSON-LD emitted: Organization (every page, stable
`@id`), BreadcrumbList (every non-root page), NewsArticle (articles), and
SportsEvent (matches). hreflang alternates plus `x-default` are reciprocal
across locales. Titles are unique and under 60 characters; meta descriptions
are unique and in the 150 to 160 range. `robots.txt` allows all and points at
the sitemap index; the RSS feed lists all articles.

## Notes for re-running

- Raw Lighthouse JSON is not committed (large, regenerated on demand). Re-run:
  `npx lighthouse@12 <url> --only-categories=performance,accessibility,best-practices,seo`.
- A full Lighthouse pass needs a Chromium-class browser, so it runs locally or
  in a dedicated CI job, not in the standard Pages build.
